## Proposed: AI Company Quality Rating System

### Concept

A two-sided iterative rating (PageRank-style) where **people are scored by career history** and **companies are scored by the people who work there**. Person quality depends on company quality and vice versa — iterate until convergence.

### Person Score — Signals

| Signal | Source | Access |
|--------|--------|--------|
| Publications, h-index, citations | Google Scholar, Semantic Scholar, DBLP | Public |
| Conference prestige (NeurIPS, ICML, ICLR vs. workshops) | Same | Public |
| Open source contributions, repo stars | GitHub API | Public |
| Career trajectory (previous employers) | LinkedIn, company pages | Partially gated |
| Role seniority (CTO/founder vs. junior) | LinkedIn, team pages | Partially gated |
| Education (institution, degree) | Scholar, LinkedIn | Mixed |
| Co-authorship network | Scholar/DBLP | Public |

**Weighting**: recency (recent > old), duration (5yr > 3mo), role level (founder > IC), early-employee bonus (employee #5 at future unicorn ≠ employee #5000).

### Company Score — Aggregation

- **Talent density** — average person score of current employees
- **Peak talent** — top-3 / top-10% mean (one world-class researcher matters)
- **Talent flow direction** — net flow of top people joining vs. leaving
- **Research output** — company-affiliated papers, patents
- **Investor quality** — tier of backers (from Crunchbase)
- **Product signal** — shipped products, revenue, customer base

### MVP Approach (No LinkedIn Required)

1. Scrape all 333 CNAIP entity detail pages for team members and descriptions
2. Search Google Scholar for affiliated researchers — aggregate h-index, citation count, top-venue publications
3. Search GitHub for company orgs — stars, forks, contributor activity
4. Pull Crunchbase funding data
5. Cross-reference job postings for hiring depth signals
6. Build bipartite person–company graph, run iterative scoring

### Expected Insights

- **Cluster analysis**: research-heavy vs. product-heavy vs. consulting
- **"Real AI" vs. "AI-washed"**: companies claiming AI but lacking technical depth
- **Talent feeder networks**: which company alumni go on to found others (Czech AI "mafia" mapping)
- **Talent flow trends**: where are top ML engineers moving to/from
